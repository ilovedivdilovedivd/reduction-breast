Will an arm lift help for crepey loose skin on a lean person?
Arm Lift and Loose Skin
Oct 3, 2017
The goal of an arm lift is to remove excess skin from the arms, not necessarily to address, correct or improve the thin, crepey surface texture of the skin that remains. However, tightening the arms by removing excess skin should result in some improvement of the thin, loose skin that you seem to have. A series of laser treatments to thicken and strengthen the skin may be a good thing to look into either after or instead of an arm lift.
Would a brachio be best with a skin tightening treatment for my excess wrinkly skin on forearm? (
Wrinkly arm skin
Oct 3, 2017
Surgery to remove excess skin would be the first thing to do. There's no point trying to laser skin that won't be there after it's cut off. An arm lift surgery would remove the skin that is loose, and a series of laser treatments to tighten/thicken the skin would benefit not only the crepey skin but also improve the scar left on your arms from the arm lift surgery.

I've lost an extreme amount of weight and my arms are extremely sagging.
Sagging Arms
Aug 22, 2017
An arm lift (or brachiplasty, as it's referred to) is the ideal procedure for people who have lost a significant amount of weight and have hanging skin of the upper arms. The procedure is generally performed under general anesthesia and the result can be a dramatic improvement in the contour of your arms. A scar is left usually along the inner aspect of the upper arm.
