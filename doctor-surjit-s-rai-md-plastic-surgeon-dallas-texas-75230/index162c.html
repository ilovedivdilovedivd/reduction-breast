Can you use insurance for a breast reduction?
Breast reduction and insurance
Apr 27, 2018
A lot of insurance providers will cover breast reductions. There are certain criteria including frequent rashes under the breasts, back pain, neck pain, shoulder pain, shoulder strap grooving...etc.
It is worth checking out with a plastic surgeon in your area that accepts your insurance to see if you are a candidate. Most surgeons would recommend you be 6 months removed from breast feeding prior to an operation as your breast size will continue to change.
Saline over or under the muscle?
Implants for smaller chests
Apr 27, 2018
Generally if you don't have a lot of natural breast tissue and know you want saline implants most surgeons would recommend placing them under the muscle. The muscle will give an extra layer of tissue between the implant and the skin and masks any rippling that can occur in saline implants.
Would it be better to have an eyelid lift before or after weight loss?
Eyelid surgery and weight loss
Apr 27, 2018
For surgery in general it is always best to get to your goal weight first. That way you maximize the results of the surgery and you are overall healthier and able to recover faster with less complications.
In regards to eyelid surgeries generally people don't gain and loose a lot of weight in this region of their face so generally speaking it can be done at any time.
2 weeks post tummy tuck and I’m so tired. Is that normal?
Recovery after a tummy tuck
Apr 27, 2018
It is completely normal to be tired for several weeks after a large operation like a tummy tuck. I tell my patients to expect to take frequent naps during the day for the first two weeks. Your body is using a lot of resources to heal your incision and you should give it the time and energy it needs to do that.
If you have any questions or concerns you should speak to your surgeon and they can better tell you what activities you should restrict.
